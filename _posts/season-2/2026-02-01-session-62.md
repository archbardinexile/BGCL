With a sunken heart, **Guy** enlists **Gomruss**, **Bantonio** and *Lothran* to crew the catamaran while the others defend *Pedn Vounder*. *Anchorheart* would undoubtedly send scouts to investigate the grotto once Guy was aboard the *Umberlee's Wrath*. When the githzechi report that Anchorheart's ships are retreating south as promised, **Firien** casts sending to *Vetra* with an alternative western escape route. Vetra warns that the submarine lost sight of Anchorheart's ships to the north—they may still be skulking around *Farwest*. **Blythe** climbs to the top of Pedn Vounder to keep watch as the catamaran sets sail.

### Umberlee's Wrath

Anchorheart greets Guy as a wayward son, squeezing the breath from his lungs as soon as he's aboard. The rotund Umberlant asks after Guy's wellbeing and fondles his enormous beard listening to Guy's stories. Unexpectedly, Anchorheart welcomes Gomruss aboard with a hearty handshake. When he shows interest in Gomruss' expertise, the dragonborn claims to be an illusionist.

> "You have no idea what I went through to get this gun, Captain. I had to hide in a mechanical horse!"
>
> "But keep it to yourself... it's clop secret!"
>
> "HAHAHAHAHAHA! Why are none of you laughing?"
>
> *Guy and Gomruss entertain Anchorheart.*

Aboard the catamaran, Bantonio sharpens his sword with a magical whetstone, fearing a confrontation may be inevitable. Gomruss observes more than twenty crewmembers on deck—they were desperately outnumbered. Fortunately, the elemental gem he had given Guy could provide adequate distraction. The dragonborn's eyes are drawn to the magical warhammer on Anchorheart's belt. Could this be the source of his power? When questioned, the Umberlant grabs Guy in a headlock and whispers in his ear.

> "I've made a deal with the devil, lad."
>
> "Which one?"
>
> "Oh, no I was speaking figuratively..."
>
> *Anchorheart hints at an alliance.*

Anchorheart's jovial tone darkens suddenly. With a wave of his hand, *Rose* is brought forward in manacles. Sparing Rose a glance, Guy recoils before Anchorheart can take the gemlock rifle. His final gamble might just lure Anchorheart to *Pedn Vounder*. He claims that the *Crosscut Circle* abandoned the submarine at the grotto when they were attacked by the mysterious denizens of an adamantine tower that had appeared overnight. Anchorheart seems intrigued. Antiquan ruins often held valuable treasures. Guy's heart pounds as the Captain mulls it over.

> "What's to stop me taking the gun and sending my ships to Pedn Vounder right now?"
>
> "You can't start the submarine without me, Captain..."
>
> "Oh? When did you become an engineer?"
>
> "I was there when it was being built. Trust me, it's unlike anything you've ever seen."
>
> *Guy deceives Anchorheart.*
